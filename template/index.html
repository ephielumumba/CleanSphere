Air, Water and soil are the essential natural resources.

The earth is the only planet that is suitable for life in this universe. Life exists because of its interaction with the resources the earth provides. It is this interaction that creates a balance between the living and the nonliving resources and sustains both of them. The three most important nonliving resources for us is the air we breathe, the water we drink and the land we live on. Without these three resources, we cannot exist. In this section, we will discuss the importance of air, water, and soil for the sustenance of living beings.

Clean environment for all.

Soil pollution is caused by the presence of chemicals or other alteration in the natural soil environment. Resulting in a change of the soil quality is likely to affect the normal use of the soil or endangering public health and the living environment. Soil is upper layer of earth crust, contains organic matter and other minerals. Dumping of wastes, garbage, rubbish like glass, plastics, metallic cans, papers, cloth rags, containers causes soil pollution. Other causes include discharge of Industrial wastes, fly ash from Thermal power plants, sewage sludge and radioactive wastes, fertilizers and pesticides on soils. Effects are reduction of soil productivity, adverse effect on soil flora and fauna, sludge contains worms, bacteria and pathogens that are detrimental to public health, radioactive wastes enter food chain, untreated products and wastewater before discharge.

    Mission Statement

    At CleanSphere, our mission is to empower individuals, communities, and organizations with real-time, data-driven insights to combat pollution and protect the planet. We aim to provide accessible, actionable information through innovative pollution tracking tools that promote environmental stewardship, inform policy decisions, and foster a global movement towards cleaner air, water, and ecosystems. Together, we strive to create a world where sustainable practices are the norm, and environmental health is prioritized for future generations.




    Our Objectives


    Track Pollution Accurately

    Leverage cutting-edge technologies like IoT sensors, satellite imagery, and AI to provide precise, real-time pollution data that identifies pollution hotspots globally.


    Empower Communities

    Engage and equip individuals, local groups, and citizen scientists to actively monitor pollution in their areas and contribute valuable data that supports environmental action.



    Advocate for Change

    Provide key data insights that help influence policymakers, drive regulatory changes, and advocate for environmental policies that reduce pollution and its harmful effects.

    Why take care of the environment?

    A clean environment directly contributes to better health. Pollution, whether in the air, water, or soil, can lead to respiratory diseases, waterborne illnesses, and many other health issues. Protecting the environment ensures cleaner air to breathe, safer water to drink, and healthier food sources.

    The environment is home to a diverse range of species, each playing an essential role in maintaining ecological balance. By protecting habitats and ecosystems, we help preserve biodiversity, ensuring the survival of plants and animals, some of which may have life-saving or life-enhancing properties for humans.
